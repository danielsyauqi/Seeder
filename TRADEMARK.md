# Trademark & Brand Policy

The **Seeder source code** is open source under the [MIT License](LICENSE) —
you're free to use, fork, modify, and redistribute it, including commercially.

The **Seeder name and brand are not.** This policy explains the line between
the two, so the community can build freely while the project's identity stays
clear and unconfusing.

## What this covers

The following are trademarks and brand assets of the authors (Daniel Syauqi and
Thaqif Rosdi), and are **not** granted by the MIT License:

- The name **"Seeder"**
- The Seeder logo and sprout glyph
- The visual identity, wordmark, and associated branding

Open-source licenses cover *copyright* (the code), not *trademarks* (the name
and brand). So even though the code is MIT, the name and logo remain ours — the
same way you can fork Chromium but can't call your build "Chrome."

## What you MAY do

- Use the code under the MIT License for anything, including commercial use.
- Refer to the project by name accurately — e.g. "built with Seeder", "a fork
  of Seeder", "compatible with Seeder", "Seeder MCP integration."
- State factually that your product is derived from or based on Seeder.
- Use the name in articles, tutorials, reviews, and comparisons.

## What you MAY NOT do without permission

- Name your fork, derivative, product, company, or service **"Seeder"** (or a
  name confusingly similar to it).
- Use the Seeder logo or wordmark as the identity of your own product or
  service.
- Use the name or logo in any way that implies your project is **official**,
  endorsed by, or affiliated with the Seeder authors when it is not.
- Register "Seeder" (or a confusingly similar mark), or any Seeder logo, as a
  trademark, domain, or app-store listing that suggests it is the official
  project.

## Forks

You're welcome — encouraged — to fork Seeder. If you publish a fork or a
derivative product, please:

1. Give it your **own distinct name** and branding.
2. Keep the original copyright notice and LICENSE intact (see [NOTICE](NOTICE)).
3. Make clear it is an **independent fork, not affiliated** with or endorsed by
   the Seeder project.

## Requesting permission

If you'd like to use the Seeder name or brand in a way this policy doesn't
allow, just ask — email **seeder.admin@gmail.com**. We're happy to grant
reasonable use; we only want to prevent confusion about what is and isn't the
official project.
